Root Cause Analysis — Findings
§ 03

Initial assessment assumed a single point of failure. Full lifecycle mapping revealed five compounding failures — each independent, each reasonable in isolation, collectively catastrophic.

FINDING-001 Column showed value — not ownership HIGH
The Lowest Bid column communicated the lowest rate on the FU. It did not communicate whether that rate belonged to the viewing financier or a competitor. When both values are identical — as in this incident — the column is technically accurate and entirely misleading at the same time. The system was answering a different question than the one the financier was asking.
FINDING-002 No notification when a counter-bid was placed HIGH
When a competing financier's bid was approved on a contested FU, the affected party received nothing. No in-app alert, no email, no visual state change. The competitive landscape changed in real time. The platform said nothing. A financier managing 20–40 FUs simultaneously had no mechanism to detect a newly contested position on any one of them.
FINDING-003 Bid status invisible during Checker Pending window CRITICAL
A bid in Checker Pending is a draft — not live, not competing. The UI presented no indication of this. The Maker saw their bid rate and had no basis to conclude it was not actively competing. This is the exact window in which the ₹8.97 Cr incident occurred: two bids sitting in separate Checker Pending queues, neither party aware the other existed.
FINDING-004 Checker approved without competitive context HIGH
The Checker's Pending Review tab displayed one thing: a bid rate. A single number. For a revision, the Checker received no indication it was a revision, no visibility into the previously approved bid still live on the platform, no current lowest bid, and no projected position if approved. Financial approval was executed with a fraction of the information required to make it.
FINDING-005 State transitions happened silently across all tabs MEDIUM
FUs moving between tabs produced no explanation of why, what bid was honored, or what happened to pending actions. Every transition was treated as a technical event. The user experienced it as a disappearance. On a regulated financial platform, unexplained disappearances are not a minor UX gap — they are a trust and audit failure.
Investigator Notes
§ 04
Naman Mudgil — Product Design Assigned 2024-10-15 · Staff Eng + PM triage confirmed: design problem

My first instinct — like everyone else's — was a notification problem. Add an alert when a counter-bid is placed. Brief the engineers. Done. I was wrong. I am glad I didn't stop there.

When I sat with the staff engineer to map the full bid lifecycle — every tab, every state, every transition for both the Maker and Checker — what emerged was not one problem but five failures that had been invisible because nobody had mapped the system end to end before.

"This was not a notification problem. It was a systematic invisibility problem. The platform had built a real-time auction engine and then left its participants flying blind inside it."

Two pushbacks shaped the final solution. The PM's initial brief was narrow: add a counter-bid notification and close the ticket. I pushed back. After mapping the five failures, I showed that a notification alone solved Finding-002 and left Findings 001, 003, 004, and 005 untouched. A financier could receive the alert, check the screen, see My Bid 5.5% and Lowest Bid 5.5%, and still not know they were losing. The scope was expanded.

The compliance constraint around competitor identity — which initially appeared to block the position indicator entirely — forced a more elegant solution. The indicator requires zero competitor data. The session already knows the financier's own approved bids. The platform already knows the lowest bid. The comparison is a binary: is this mine or not? No competitor name. No rate. No identifying information of any kind.

One edge case was caught during revision flow mapping that had no ticket against it: if a buyer closes an auction while a revised bid is in Checker Pending, the system correctly honors the last confirmed approved bid — but the Bid Lost tab was showing the draft revision value. A financier could lose an FU and see a bid rate on their screen that the platform never treated as real. Flagged as P0 before the revision flow went to development. Not in the original brief. Would have shipped undetected.

Resolution — Applied Fixes
§ 05

Three solution layers. Each maps directly to specific findings. No fix is decorative.

FIX-001 Bid Position Indicator — Four-State Signal SHIPPED
Resolves FINDING-001 · FINDING-003 (partial)
The Lowest Bid column redesigned from a value display into a competitive position signal. System compares the financier's own approved bid against the platform lowest bid and returns one of four states. Zero competitor data exposed at any layer. Backend requirement: one four-state position flag per FU per session — LEADING, MATCHED_FIRST, MATCHED_NOT_FIRST, TRAILING. Data to compute it already existed. No new data pipeline required.
LEADING·Sole bidder — no other approved bids exist on this FU
LEADING·Matched, first timestamp — equal bid exists, you hold earliest approval
MATCHED·Not first timestamp — an earlier financier exists at this same rate
TRAILING·A lower approved bid exists — you are not leading
INLINE RULE SURFACE — "Equal bids are awarded to the earliest approved bid." Shown directly on equal-bid states. Not in a tooltip. Not in a help article. Not behind a hover. At the exact moment the rule matters, on the exact screen where it is needed.
FIX-002 Counter-bid Notification — Dual Channel, Checker-Triggered SHIPPED
Resolves FINDING-002
Trigger: a competitor's Checker approves a bid on an FU where the financier holds an active approved bid. Notification fires to both Maker and Checker independently. Trigger is Checker approval — not Maker placement. A Maker-placed bid is a draft. Notifying on placement would generate alerts for bids that never become real — flooding the channel and training participants to ignore it. The notification needs to land with weight. That only works if it is not competing with noise.
→ Maker alert
Which FU, current competitive position, reminder that any revision requires Checker re-approval before going live on platform.
→ Checker alert
Preparatory signal — counter-bid occurred on an FU they approved. Maker may submit revision. Primes Checker before the revision arrives, reducing approval lag at the most time-sensitive moment.
FIX-003 Real-time Attention Hierarchy — Four-Level Cascade SHIPPED
Resolves FINDING-002 (partial) · FINDING-003 (partial)
The Lowest Bid column already updated in real time at the row level. The update was a silent number swap — too fast to register while the Maker was scanning other rows. Real-time data that updates silently is not a feature. It is noise. Designed a four-level attention cascade so the signal finds the user regardless of where they are looking in the interface.
LEVEL 1·Cell flashes amber when Lowest Bid value changes — immediate, local signal
LEVEL 2·Row holds persistent visual state if position deteriorates — does not silently return to normal
LEVEL 3·Pagination flags pages where competitive events occurred on FUs not currently in view
LEVEL 4·Tab carries counter badge of FUs needing attention — visible from anywhere in the session
Platform Rule Established
§ 06

Midway through fix design, the investigation identified a systemic pattern underlying all five findings. Individual fixes were expanded into a platform-wide rule to prevent recurrence across all future features. The scope expansion was argued and approved mid-sprint.

Platform UX Standard · Established 2024-11 · Scope: all tabs, both personas, all state transitions, now and future
"Every time an FU moves between tabs or disappears from a tab, the system must never let it transition silently."
Every state change must answer three questions:
Why did this move? — Reason for every tab transition, surfaced as a toast message
What was honored? — The confirmed bid explicitly shown, never implied or omitted
What happened to pending actions? — No pending action disappears without a documented resolution
